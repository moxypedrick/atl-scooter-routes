Over the course of 2 weeks, we recorded the location of every idle e-scooter in the Atlanta area. Using this data we created a database of unique origins and destinations for each scooter at a 5 minute resolution. We determined the most likely routes between each origin and destination using the HERE.com bike routing API. Each route was assigned to a street segement to determine which streets are used most frequently with shared e-scooters.

This data is road segment counts are visualized with existing and planned light-individual-transportation lanes. Hopefully, this will help inform decision makers of where to implement their accelerated LIT lane infrastructure to maximize e-scooter safety.

The data visualized was recorded between October 16th and November 1, 2019. The data was recorded from 4am to 9:30 pm, reflecting the Atlanta's nighttime curfew. During data collection, I experienced an internet outage on October 22, so data is missing. Additionaly, data collection for Lyft and Lime scooters was not captured due to unknown difficulties with the API.
