Over the course of 16 days, we recorded the idle location of every e-scooter in the Atlanta area. Using this data we created a database of every e-scooter trip that occurred. We utilized the HERE.com bike routing API to determine the likely route traveled on each trip. Then each route was assigned to a street segement to determine which streets are used most frequently with shared e-scooters.

This data is a visualization of the frequency of e-scooter use on each road segment in Atlanta. We also provide the existing and planned light-individual-transportation lanes to juxtapose planned infrastructure and current e-scooter use. Hopefully, this will help inform decision makers of where to implement accelerated LIT lane infrastructure to maximize e-scooter safety.

The data visualized was observed between October 16th and November 1, 2019. The data was recorded from 4am to 9:30 pm, reflecting the Atlanta's nighttime curfew. During data collection, we experienced an internet outage on October 22, so these data are missing. Additionaly, data collection for Lyft and Lime scooters was not captured due to unknown difficulties with the API.
